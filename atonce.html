    Overview

    AtOnce is an early stage start-up helping connect international students, service providers and education agents via an online marketplace. They had an existing platform built using a no-code tool which served as their MVP and were now seeking to uplift the platform and improve the user experience.

    The Challenge

    Improve the listing upload experience

    The MVP to date had largely been designed by junior low-code developers with limited UX knowledge, leaving disparate features and usability issues throughout, ultimately resulting in users struggling to navigate the platform. Our brief was focussed on a key part of the user flow; to improve the user experience of a service provider uploading / advertising their services on the AtOnce platform.
    This client project was completed as part of Academy Xi‚Äôs UX / UI Design Bootcamp. We worked in a team comprising 5 UX designers, and worked remotely across both Melbourne and Sydney.
    We set up working norms as a team from the outset and each had area‚Äôs of the design process we were specifically responsible for, however everyone in the team was consulted and accountable at every stage throughout the process.

    Let's dive in!

    Discover

    Understanding the problem

    The team and I began discovery by outlining the key goals we had for this phase, which were to understand:

    1. The wider problem space
    2. The needs and challenges of service providers when it comes to marketplace advertising
    3. How service providers partner with education agents
    4. How education agents refer their international students to service providers

    We conducted user interviews with both education agents and service providers to learn more. We also sat down with the AtOnce intern / development team to gain some insight into their learnings so far. These conversations led to us uncover some key insights that would later form the basis of our solution:

    Service Providers:

    • Uploading advertisements or listings is often a long, arduous process
    • The ad needs to look attractive and include details that are specific to their particular service
    • They want to see ROI. They want confidence their ad going to get them results.

    Education Agents:

    • The agents‚Äô reputation is on the line if they refer a students to a poor provider
    • They need to compare multiple service providers against each other
    • Agents choose to re-use their existing partnerships over and over.

    Learning from competitors

    We also wanted to understand the current landscape of other marketplace platforms, to gain insight into the things they do well (or not so well) and to identify common design patterns and IA we can utilise, as these will already be familiar to our users and will reduce friction and cognitive load.

    We conducted competitor analyses into four different marketplaces, looking closely at the listing / advertisement upload flow and the design of the final listing. In particular we analysed Facebook Ads, Gumtree, Airbnb & Flatmates.

    Define

    Reducing Scope

    During our competitor research, we realised that the listing creation experience varied greatly depending on the category of the service you‚Äôre that you‚Äôre creating a listing for.

    Given our short project timeline it was going to be unrealistic for us to effectively address the listing needs for all of the service provider types that will be using the AtOnce platform. The decision was made to hone in on a single service provider type; accommodation providers.

    Why accommodation providers?

    Accommodation is a non-negotiable for an international student arriving in Australia. Whilst student needs can vary widely, we can assume that close to everyone needs a place to stay when they move countries. We also hypothesised that due to the high value nature of the transaction (e.g. rent), there would be larger opportunity to capture revenue share from service providers (e.g. commission).

    Given these factors, we felt this was the highest value item for both the client and the users of the platform. With more time, we would have completed this UX process for each service provider type.

This can help us pinpoint where there are opportunities for improvement, and where we can turn a currently challenging experience into a positive one.

    Develop

    Ideating a solution

    With insights from our user interviews, heuristics analysis and journey mapping we had a thorough understanding of the pain points our users were facing - now we had to think about how to solve them! In order to do this we held a brainstorming session as a team, putting together multiple ‚Äòhow might we‚Äô statements to re-frame the problems we‚Äôre aiming to solve and encourage creativity.

    Following the session, we plotted our solution ideas on an Impact x Effort matrix to help structure our prioritisation. We were conscious of the short timeline we had and so focussed on the Low effort, High impact quick wins that we identified.

    This lead to the prioritisation of the following solutions:

    • Having a staged upload process, displaying the users progress
    • Increase iconography to help reduce text
    • Introduction screen with details outlining the time and effort commitment to the user
    • Testimonials & ratings
    • Pre-defined options to select from + Industry specific
    • Ability to upload a hero listing image + listing preview

    Determining the information architecture

This was particularly important as we began thinking about the ordering, groupings and hierarchy of the content in the listing upload flow.

    Deliver

    Mapping out the user flow and wireframes

    Before jumping into design we mapped out the user flow of our users from the point they enter the platform to when their ad is successfully uploaded.

    With our proposed direction involving multiple screens, it was important that a user could go back to previous steps and even pause the process half way through if required. This prompted the introduction of a ‚Äòsave as draft‚Äô workflow, and clarified the entry and exit points into the journey.

    We then put together some basic wireframes of our proposed solution, taking our defined features from the MVP matrix, the information architecture from the card sorting and the learnings from our competitor research. Our wireframes helped us get a better sense of the structure and hierarchy of our screens and be a final check on any potential gaps that may have been missed before moving on to prototyping.

    Bringing it to life

    Confident with our design direction, we moved into designing the high-fidelity prototype that we would use to test with users. This included thinking through the specific selection option details and using AtOnce‚Äôs brand guidelines to produce a professional concept.

    Validating the designs

    We conducted usability testing sessions with accommodation providers to validate whether the new designs would solve the challenges they face. We put together a scenario asking the user to create a new listing and observed how they interacted with the prototype.

    The feedback from the session was very positive, revealing that uploading a listing was much more straightforward and simple to do. The language was relevant and the fields and screens were easy to navigate for the users. However some needs were also highlighted:


    • Integration with third party review sources to reduce manual entry and potential bias
    • Tracking ROI on listings

    • Implement solution for additional service provider types
    • Revamp the marketplace view for buyers
